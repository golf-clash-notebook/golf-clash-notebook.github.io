The Backbone

Wind Chart Wind Chart
The Backbone is a solid long iron to start off and starts to get pretty good once you get it to level 9. Thankfully it's a common so that won't take as long as other options. Most people will play this club or the Goliath on their way to the top. It all depends on whether you prefer distance or accuracy. In all the other meaningful long iron stats, they're pretty close.

The B52

Wind Chart Wind Chart
Arguably the best long iron once you get it maxed. It lacks spin control when compared to other clubs which limits it's usefulness. Think about using this club at level 3+ for longer Par 3 tournament holes where spin isn't necessary but distance is. In those situations, it can often be a better option than the Goliath because it's just as long, more accurate and comes with a really nice ball guide.

The Tsunami

Wind Chart Wind Chart
Can't really talk about using this one until it hits at least level 5. It's still not a club that you can just pick up an be effective with at that point though because of the lack of ball guide. It's really strong in all the other categories though so if you have the discipline or sheer talent you can make this club do some amazing things. I would highly recommend watching Zachary Jones' extended tutorial on this club if you're thinking about using this one or even if you already are.
